Privacy Policy

At NodeZero Recruitment, we respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or communicate with us as a client or candidate.

1. How we handle your data

We process personal data in accordance with applicable data protection laws, including the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

2. What data we collect

Depending on how you interact with us, we may collect the following categories of personal data:

  • Identification data, such as your name and contact details.
  • Professional data, such as your CV, work history, skills, portfolio links, and role preferences.
  • Client and assignment data, including company name, contact persons, hiring needs, team setup, and role requirements.
  • Communication data, such as emails, messages, call or meeting notes, and website contact form submissions.

We only collect personal data that is relevant and necessary for recruitment and related communication purposes.

We do not collect or process any Special Categories of Personal Data within the meaning of Article 9 of the GDPR. This includes data relating to racial or ethnic origin, religious or philosophical beliefs, sexual orientation or sex life, political opinions, trade union membership, health, genetic data, or biometric data.

We also do not process personal data relating to criminal convictions or offences under Article 10 of the GDPR.

3. How we use your data and lawful basis

We process personal data for the following purposes and lawful bases under Article 6 of the GDPR:

  • Recruitment and matching of candidates with suitable roles and clients (legitimate interest).
  • Communication regarding opportunities, recruitment processes, and next steps (legitimate interest or consent, depending on context).
  • Managing client relationships and service delivery (performance of a contract or legitimate interest).
  • Compliance with legal or regulatory obligations, where applicable (legal obligation).

We do not use personal data for automated decision-making or profiling within the meaning of Article 22 of the GDPR.

4. Web forms and third parties

When you submit a message through our website contact form, your data is transmitted via Web3Forms, which processes the submission on our behalf so that we can receive and respond to your inquiry.

We only include form fields that are necessary to respond to your request or explore relevant opportunities. We recommend that you do not include sensitive personal information in free-text fields.

Where third-party service providers are used, they act as data processors and are contractually required to process personal data in accordance with GDPR requirements.

Where personal data is processed outside the European Economic Area, appropriate safeguards are applied in accordance with GDPR requirements.

5. Data retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to carry out recruitment and placement activities, manage ongoing client or candidate relationships, or comply with applicable legal obligations.

You may request deletion of your personal data at any time. We will comply unless retention is required by law or for legitimate business purposes.

6. Your rights under the GDPR

Under the GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Request access to your personal data.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Request deletion of your data.
  • Request restriction of processing.
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Request data portability, where applicable.
  • Withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on consent.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details on our contact page. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority if you believe your data is being processed unlawfully.

7. Data security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, misuse, or disclosure.

8. Contact

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle personal data, please contact us via our website contact page or the contact details provided at the top of this Privacy Policy.
